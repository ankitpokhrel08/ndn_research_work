What the timeseries plots confirm:
IFA signature (clean and clear):

satisfaction_ratio drops hard at t=300 (collapses to near 0)
timeout_ratio spikes hard at t=300 (jumps to 0.6+)
InInterests spikes massively at t=300
Signal is sustained and clean after t=300

CP signature (completely different and noisy):

satisfaction_ratio INCREASES after t=300 (goes UP not down)
timeout_ratio DROPS and OSCILLATES — periodic bursts up and down
cache_hit_ratio DROPS slightly but also oscillates
InInterests increases moderately but steadily
The bursting pattern you see is the 100 junk streams completing their requests in waves

The core problem I now understand fully:
The IFA and CP attacks produce opposite signatures in the ratio features. Our current detector uses the same features and same direction for both attacks. That's why CP either doesn't trigger or triggers at the wrong time.
What the KS heatmaps tell us:
For IFA the separating nodes are 0-5 (routers in dfn/tree) and specific consumer nodes. For CP the separating nodes are different — notably nodes 6 and 11 (consumers, the actual attackers c1 and c6) show the strongest KS scores for cache_hit_ratio and satisfaction_ratio.
The bursting in CP timeout_ratio is the key insight. The 100 junk streams at 2/s each complete, then the interest lifetime expires, then new interests go out. This creates a periodic cycle not a sustained shift. That's why our persistence filter doesn't work for CP — the signal never persists for 3 consecutive steps.
What the boxplots confirm:
In CP, satisfaction_ratio and timeout_ratio distributions for IFA and CP overlap heavily with normal when averaged across all nodes. But cache_hit_ratio on consumer nodes 6 and 11 (the attackers) shows clear separation — they drop from the normal baseline.
My diagnosis — three separate problems:
Problem 1: Wrong features for CP. satisfaction_ratio and timeout_ratio go in opposite directions for CP vs IFA. Using them together cancels out. For CP the only clean signal is cache_hit_ratio DROP on attacker-adjacent nodes and InInterests increase.
Problem 2: Wrong persistence assumption for CP. CP produces oscillating bursts not sustained deviations. A persistence filter of 3 consecutive steps will miss most of the burst windows.
Problem 3: IFA pre-300 signal is already elevated. From the timeseries you can see IFA and normal diverge even before t=300 — the IFA scenario starts differently from normal. This means the baseline built from the first 200s of the IFA file is itself abnormal for some nodes.
My proposed fix — before writing any code:
Separate the detection into two independent detectors:
IFA detector: uses satisfaction_ratio (downward) + timeout_ratio (upward) + InInterests (upward). Persistence = 3 steps. Strong sustained signal.
CP detector: uses cache_hit_ratio (downward) + InInterests (upward, moderate). Persistence = 1 step with a longer observation window (flag if >30% of last 10 steps are anomalous). This handles the oscillating burst pattern.
Combined score: max(IFA_score, CP_score) per node per timestep.
The baseline for both should come from the pure normal scenario files only, not from the first 200s of attack scenario files. This fixes the pre-300 contamination issue.
